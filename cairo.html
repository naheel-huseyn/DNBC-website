Business

Cairo’s business environment is currently experiencing a surge in growth and transformation driven by major investments and urban development. A landmark $35 billion deal with the United Arab Emirates is set to turn the Ras El Hekma region into a premier hub for tourism, commerce, and real estate, potentially drawing up to $150 billion in total investments. The city also recently hosted a major Saudi-Egyptian Business Forum, resulting in new agreements and deeper economic collaboration across sectors like food security, infrastructure, and tourism. Domestically, initiatives such as the “Giza 2030” project and the revitalization of Downtown Cairo are reshaping the city’s infrastructure and enhancing its appeal to both investors and tourists. On the financial front, Egypt has secured $2.5 billion in IMF support, leading to improved economic stability and a significant reduction in inflation rates. Meanwhile, Cairo is also advancing its green economy with investments in green hydrogen projects and the development of the 35-kilometer Green River Park — poised to become the world’s longest urban park — signaling the city’s commitment to sustainable growth and innovation.
