The introduction of an AI-powered Darth Vader in Fortnite has sparked controversy, leading the Screen Actors Guild-American Federation of Television and Radio Artists (SAG-AFTRA) to file an unfair labor practice charge against Epic Games. The union alleges that Llama Productions, a subsidiary of Epic, utilized artificial intelligence to replicate the iconic voice of Darth Vader, previously portrayed by James Earl Jones, without prior consultation or negotiation with the union. SAG-AFTRA contends that this action bypasses established labor agreements and replaces the work of human performers with AI technology, violating fair labor practices.This case also raises questions about the future of labor agreements in an era where AI can mimic human performances with increasing accuracy. SAG-AFTRA fears that without clear regulations, studios and game developers might increasingly rely on AI to replace actors, thereby undermining jobs and reducing creative professionals’ bargaining power.

Despite the estate of James Earl Jones granting permission for the use of his voice in the game, SAG-AFTRA emphasizes the importance of protecting performers' rights to negotiate terms regarding the use of their digital likenesses. The union argues that such unilateral decisions undermine the collective bargaining process and set a concerning precedent for the use of AI in replacing human performers. This complaint highlights the growing tensions between technological advancements and labor rights in the entertainment industry, particularly concerning the use of AI to replicate voices and likenesses without appropriate compensation or consent.The Screen Actors Guild-American Federation of Television and Radio Artists (SAG-AFTRA) argues that AI tools are being used to replicate iconic voices without proper consent or compensation to the original performers or their estates. Even though James Earl Jones’s estate gave permission for the use of his voice likeness, the union insists that any such use must be negotiated collectively with the actors’ union to protect performers’ rights and prevent exploitation.
