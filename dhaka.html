Business

Dhaka’s business environment in 2025 is shaped by critical economic reforms, international financial support, and ongoing infrastructure development amid political changes. The IMF has approved a $1.3 billion loan to support reforms like exchange rate adjustments and tax system restructuring, although Bangladesh faces a projected GDP slowdown to 3.3% due to investment declines and political uncertainty. Key infrastructure projects like the Dhaka Elevated Expressway and Metro Rail are progressing, despite some delays from protests. The garment sector is adapting to global trade challenges by increasing U.S. cotton imports to offset tariffs. Politically, significant shifts are underway with the banning of major parties, adding complexity to the economic outlook. Overall, Dhaka is balancing reform efforts and development while navigating a dynamic and sometimes uncertain political and economic landscape.
